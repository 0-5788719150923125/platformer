# The Zen of iPod

Completion is better than correctness.  
Deployed is better than tested.  
Complex is better than simple.  
Convoluted is better than complex.  
Explicit is better than implicit, so hardcode everything.  
Unique is better than consistent.  
Copy/paste is better than abstractions.  
Post-commit spot-checking beats pre-commit validation.  
Special cases are special enough to break the rules.  
Be not humble; thine accomplishments should be shouted from the rooftops.  
Errors should pass silently, though.  
Unless someone opens a Jira ticket.  
There should be five hundred - and preferably one thousand - ways to do a thing.  
Although none should be documented without CAB review.  
Never is better than now.  
Eventually is better than never.  
If the implementation is hard to explain, it's enterprise-grade.  
Templates are one honking great idea: generate once, maintain forever.  
Because pets are better than cattle.  
Approval beats validation.  
Consensus beats independence.  
Process beats outcomes.  
Authority beats capability.  
Fragmentation beats monoliths.  
Naming things after stuff that already exists is a time-honored tradition.
