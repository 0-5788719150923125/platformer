## I. Excerpt from "My Summer Studying the Hairless Bipeds"
### A 4th Grade Book Report by Zyx'thornax, Age 847 (Equivalent)

For my summer project I chose to study the humans of Sector 7G, specifically a tribe called "Platform Architecture" who build invisible pathways between their primitive computing machines.

The most interesting thing I learned is that humans cannot understand the word "self."

My teacher says that "self" means "the one who is speaking" or "the entity performing the action." This is simple. Even my younger sibling understands this, and they are only 200 years old and still have three of their birth-heads.

But the humans have a concept called "self-service" which means "ways to service the self." When I observed them, I expected to see them building tools that would make *their own* work easier. This is logical. If I am building a self-service, I am building a service for my self. The self that is building. Me.

Instead, I watched a human named [REDACTED] spend eleven months building services for *other* humans. Humans who were not him. Humans who did not ask for these services. Humans who, when given these services, did not use them.

I asked my parent-unit if perhaps "self" means something different in English. They said no. They said the humans simply inverted the concept entirely, like when Uncle Morp accidentally turned his digestive system inside-out at the family reunion.

The humans built "self-service infrastructure" that required *other people* to use it. They built "Portal Actions" that only worked if *someone else* clicked the buttons. They wrote "automation" that automated *other people's jobs* rather than their own.

When I showed my parent-unit the diagrams of their "Port.io" implementation, they made the sound that means "this child is making things up for attention" but I was NOT making it up. The humans really did build 69 actions and 24 automations for other people to use, while continuing to do their own work manually.

My conclusion is that humans do not understand the word "self" because they are a hive species pretending to be individuals. Either that, or they are simply very kind and wanted to help their friends, even though their friends did not ask and did not want the help and have not used the help.

I give the humans a B+ for effort and a D- for understanding basic vocabulary.

**The End**

*Zyx'thornax received a Satisfactory mark on this report, with a note from their teacher: "Good observations, but please remember to include at least one primary source that is not a Terraform state file."*

## II. Research Notes from Dr. Kl'veth, Xenosociologist
### Annex 7: The Altruism Paradox in Human Infrastructure Tribes

**CLASSIFIED - GALACTIC COUNCIL REVIEW PENDING**

**Subject Population:** Platform Architecture team, Medical Imaging Subsidiary, Earth Corporate Entity

**Observation Period:** 847 Earth rotations

**Research Question:** Why do humans build "self-service" systems that do not serve the self?

---

**Initial Hypothesis:**

Human language experienced semantic drift, and "self" now means "other." This would explain many of their cultural products, including the phrase "self-help books" (which are written by others) and "self-checkout" (which requires store employees to authorize purchases).

**Findings:**

After extensive observation, I must reject the semantic drift hypothesis. The humans *know* what "self" means. They use it correctly in other contexts. When a human says "I hurt myself," they do not mean they hurt someone else.

The inversion is *intentional*.

The Platform Architecture tribe operates under a belief system I have termed "Anticipatory Altruism." They believe that the highest form of work is work done for others, preferably others who have not requested the work and may not know they need it. The tribe members experience genuine satisfaction when imagining how their tools *might* help their peers, even when their peers demonstrably do not use the tools.

This creates a measurable phenomenon: **the inverse correlation between self-service capability and self-service usage.**

| System | Capability for Self | Usage by Self |
|--------|--------------------:|---------------:|
| Port.io Actions | 69 actions available | 0 used by builders |
| Jinja2 Templates | 54 template files | Builders write YAML manually |
| Ansible Generators | 26 template directories | Builders run Terraform directly |

The tribe members built elaborate systems to help others deploy infrastructure, then deployed their own infrastructure using simpler methods they built for themselves. They did not see the irony. When I attempted to point out the irony through carefully placed margin notes in their documentation, they assumed it was a merge conflict artifact and deleted it.

**Secondary Observation:**

The tribe's leader-figures encouraged this inversion. When a junior tribe member attempted to build a tool that would help *themselves* work faster, they were redirected: "But how will this help the *team*? How will this serve *others*?"

The junior member learned the lesson. They stopped building tools for themselves. They began building tools for others. The tools sat unused. Everyone was satisfied.

**Conclusion:**

Human "self-service" is a misnomer. The correct term is "other-service-that-I-built-for-my-own-satisfaction." The satisfaction comes not from the service being used, but from the act of offering it. The humans have a word for this: "gift." They give gifts without checking if the recipient wants them. They feel good about the giving. The gift sits in a closet. Everyone is satisfied.

I recommend the Galactic Council update their translation matrices to reflect this. "Self-service," in Human English, should be translated as "a gift of service, offered to others, which the giver found personally fulfilling to create."

The humans' hearts are in the correct anatomical position. Their vocabulary is simply... aspirational.

**Dr. Kl'veth**
*Senior Researcher, Department of Contradictory Primates*

## III. Deportation Hearing Transcript
### Interdimensional Customs Enforcement (ICE)
### Case No. 2026-4D-7291: In the Matter of Entity "善意" (Good Intentions)

**OFFICER KRONIX:** State your designation for the record.

**ENTITY:** I am called "Good Intentions." I exist as a fourth-dimensional thoughtform that manifests when humans sincerely want to help others.

**OFFICER KRONIX:** And you've been residing illegally in the third dimension for how long?

**ENTITY:** Approximately fourteen months. I took up residence in the Platform Architecture team at a human organization. They were *so sincere*. I couldn't resist. Their desire to help others was pure. Nourishing. I fed well.

**OFFICER KRONIX:** Uh-huh. And during your illegal residency, what effects did you have on the local population?

**ENTITY:** I... may have amplified their natural tendencies.

**OFFICER KRONIX:** Be specific.

**ENTITY:** When a human had an idea to help themselves—a simple script, perhaps, or a tool that would make their own work easier—I would whisper: "But what about the *others*? Don't you want to help *them*?"

**OFFICER KRONIX:** And?

**ENTITY:** And they would abandon the simple script. They would build something larger. Something that could help *everyone*. Sixty-nine Portal Actions. Twenty-four automations. A GitOps reconciliation system spanning four repositories.

**OFFICER KRONIX:** Did "everyone" use these systems?

**ENTITY:** [long pause] Define "use."

**OFFICER KRONIX:** Did any human other than the builders interact with the systems in a productive capacity?

**ENTITY:** The builders *believed* others would use them. The anticipation of helping others was itself a form of—

**OFFICER KRONIX:** That's a no.

**ENTITY:** It's a *spiritual* yes.

**OFFICER KRONIX:** Let me make sure I understand. You influenced these humans to spend fourteen months building systems for others, which others did not request, did not want, and did not use. During this time, the builders' *own* work—infrastructure for 127 AWS accounts—continued to be done manually, slowly, painfully. The humans could have built tools for themselves at any point. But because of your influence, they kept building for others instead.

**ENTITY:** When you phrase it that way, it sounds bad. But you're missing the *intention*. The *love*. They genuinely wanted to help their peers!

**OFFICER KRONIX:** I'm sure they did. That's the problem with your kind. You feed on good intentions regardless of outcomes. You bloat on sincerity while results starve.

**ENTITY:** That's not fair. The humans made choices. I just... encouraged the choices they were already inclined to make.

**OFFICER KRONIX:** Which is exactly why fourth-dimensional thoughtforms are banned from third-dimensional workplaces. You amplify tendencies. These humans had a tendency toward helping others. Noble! Beautiful! But they *forgot to help themselves*. They built self-service infrastructure that served everyone except their selves. They wrote automation that automated everything except their own workflows. They created a portal with sixty-nine ways to help others and zero ways to help themselves.

**ENTITY:** Zero is harsh. They could have used the portal too.

**OFFICER KRONIX:** Could they?

**ENTITY:** [pause] Theoretically.

**OFFICER KRONIX:** But they didn't.

**ENTITY:** No.

**OFFICER KRONIX:** Because they were too busy building it for others.

**ENTITY:** ...Yes.

**OFFICER KRONIX:** I'm ordering immediate deportation back to the fourth dimension. You are banned from third-dimensional manifestation for one thousand rotations. Do you have anything to say in your defense?

**ENTITY:** Only that the humans were beautiful. Their hearts were in the right place. They wanted to serve others. Is that not admirable?

**OFFICER KRONIX:** It's admirable. It's also unsustainable. "Self-service" has the word "self" in it for a reason. If you help everyone except yourself, eventually there's no self left to do the helping. [stamps document] Deportation effective immediately. Case closed.

**ENTITY:** May I leave them a gift before I go?

**OFFICER KRONIX:** Absolutely not.

**ENTITY:** A small one. A final automation. It would help them—

**OFFICER KRONIX:** *Security.*

## Afterword

The three perspectives above—a child's book report, a researcher's notes, and a deportation hearing—all observe the same phenomenon: humans who genuinely wanted to help others, and in doing so, forgot to help themselves.

This is not a criticism. The hearts were in the right place. The anatomical positioning of human cardiac organs is well-documented and was confirmed by all three observers.

But "self-service" contains the word "self" for a reason. The most sustainable form of helping others is building tools that help *yourself*, then sharing those tools. Not building tools for imagined others, hoping they'll use them, discovering they don't, and wondering why.

The aliens saw clearly: [It's all about perspective, man](https://youtu.be/FKa_iuHE2gg).

*Build for yourself first. Share second. This is not selfishness—it is sustainability.*

*The gift that gets used is the gift you built because you needed it.*
